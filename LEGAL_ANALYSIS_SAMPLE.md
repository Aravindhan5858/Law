## 2. APPLICABLE LEGAL SECTIONS

### **2.1 Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Section 420**
**Title:** Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property

**Description:** Whoever cheats and thereby dishonestly induces the person deceived to deliver any property to any person, shall be punished with imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.

**Relevance:** Applicable when seller takes payment but intentionally fails to deliver product and refuses refund, showing dishonest intention from the beginning.

---

### **2.2 Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Section 406**
**Title:** Criminal breach of trust

**Description:** Whoever commits criminal breach of trust shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both.

**Relevance:** Money was entrusted to seller for delivery of goods. Failure to deliver or return money constitutes breach of trust.

---

### **2.3 Information Technology Act, 2000 - Section 66D**
**Title:** Punishment for cheating by personation using computer resource

**Description:** Whoever cheats by personation using computer resource shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs. 1 lakh.

**Relevance:** E-commerce platform used as computer resource to commit fraud. Applicable if site misrepresented itself or used fake identity.

---

### **2.4 Consumer Protection Act, 2019 - Section 2(7)**
**Title:** Deficiency in Service

**Description:** Any fault, imperfection, shortcoming or inadequacy in the quality, nature and manner of performance required to be maintained by law.

**Relevance:** Complete failure to deliver product after payment is gross deficiency in service. Entitles consumer to compensation.

---

### **2.5 Consumer Protection Act, 2019 - Section 2(47)**
**Title:** Unfair Trade Practice

**Description:** A trade practice which adopts unfair methods or deceptive practices including non-performance of promise.

**Relevance:** Taking payment without delivering goods is unfair trade practice. Consumer forum can order refund with interest and compensation.

---

### **2.6 Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Section 73**
**Title:** Compensation for loss or damage caused by breach of contract

**Description:** Party breaking contract must compensate other party for any loss or damage caused which naturally arose in usual course of things from such breach.

**Relevance:** Breach of contract by non-delivery. Buyer entitled to refund plus compensation for loss suffered.

## 3. PUNISHMENT DETAILS

### **3.1 Criminal Penalties**
- **Section 420 IPC:** Imprisonment up to **7 years** + Fine
- **Section 406 IPC:** Imprisonment up to **3 years** + Fine
- **Section 66D IT Act:** Imprisonment up to **3 years** + Fine up to **Rs. 1 lakh**

**Note:** These are criminal charges that can lead to arrest and prosecution of the seller/company officials.

---

### **3.2 Civil Remedies**
**Consumer Protection Act:** Consumer forum can order:
- Full refund with interest
- Compensation for mental harassment (typically **Rs. 10,000 - Rs. 50,000**)
- Litigation costs

---

### **3.3 Compensation Breakdown**
1. **Full refund** of amount paid
2. **Interest @ 9-12%** per annum from date of payment
3. **Compensation for mental agony:** Rs. 10,000 - Rs. 50,000
4. **Litigation costs:** Rs. 5,000 - Rs. 25,000
5. **Punitive damages** (in severe cases): Up to 2x the transaction amount

## 4. RECOMMENDED ACTION STEPS

### **Step 1: Preserve All Evidence**
**Timeline:** Immediately  
**Authority:** Self  
**Action:** Take screenshots of order confirmation, payment receipt, website, emails, chat conversations, terms & conditions. Save all communication.

---

### **Step 2: Send Legal Notice**
**Timeline:** 0-3 days  
**Authority:** Lawyer (optional but recommended)  
**Action:** Send formal legal notice via registered post and email demanding refund within 15 days, failing which legal action will be initiated.

---

### **Step 3: File Consumer Complaint**
**Timeline:** 3-7 days (if no response to notice)  
**Authority:** Consumer Court  
**Action:** File complaint in District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission if amount is up to Rs. 1 crore. Can be filed online at **edaakhil.nic.in**

---

### **Step 4: File Cyber Crime Complaint**
**Timeline:** Simultaneously with consumer complaint  
**Authority:** Cyber Crime Cell / Police  
**Action:** Register FIR/complaint at National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (**cybercrime.gov.in**) and local cyber cell.

---

### **Step 5: Report to Payment Gateway**
**Timeline:** Within 7 days  
**Authority:** Bank / Payment Gateway  
**Action:** If payment made via credit card/UPI, raise chargeback dispute with bank/payment provider within 60-90 days.

---

### **Step 6: Report to Platform**
**Timeline:** Within 3 days  
**Authority:** E-commerce Platform / National Consumer Helpline  
**Action:** If purchased via marketplace (Amazon, Flipkart), file complaint with platform's grievance officer and escalate to Consumer Helpline (**1915**).

---

### **Step 7: Attend Hearings**
**Timeline:** 30-180 days after filing  
**Authority:** Consumer Court  
**Action:** Attend consumer court hearings (usually 3-6 hearings). Can appear in person or through lawyer.
